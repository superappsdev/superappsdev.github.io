Stories — Account & data deletion

Developer: superappsdev

Delete your account (in the app)

Account deletion is completed inside the Stories app. After deletion you will be signed out and will need a new account to use the app again.

  1. Open the Stories app and sign in if prompted.
  2. Open the Settings tab (bottom navigation).
  3. Tap Profile.
  4. Tap Account Actions.
  5. Tap Delete Account and confirm.
  6. If asked, complete re-authentication (enter your password for email sign-in, or complete the Google sign-in flow) — this is required for security before the account is removed.

If you cannot complete deletion in the app (for example you lost access), email superappsdev@gmail.com from the address associated with your account, ask for account deletion, and we will verify and process your request in a reasonable time.

What is removed when you delete your account

  • Your Firebase Authentication account (you can no longer sign in with that identity).
  • Your main user profile document stored in our database for the app (including fields such as display name, email on file where applicable, bookmarks, likes list, notification token, and subscription flags as stored in that profile).

Google Play: Records of purchases and subscriptions are held by Google according to Google’s policies; deleting the app account does not erase Google’s billing history.

Advertising: Ad networks (for example Google AdMob) may retain aggregated or device-level data under their own policies. See our Privacy Policy for third parties.

Remove app data without deleting your account

You can clear most usage data while keeping your login:

  1. Settings → Profile → Account Actions → Remove All Data.

This removes items such as reading history, liked stories, bookmarks, and related reading progress in the way the app implements that action.

A small amount of account data used only to run offers and limits fairly may not be reset by this action. Your reading library and history clear as described in the app when you confirm.
